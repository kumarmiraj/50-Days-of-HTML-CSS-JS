Amazing Jasper

Miraj Kumar

A trip to Jasper National Park in May 2025 is to witness a world in dramatic transition. It’s the secret shoulder season, where spring awakens in the valleys while winter holds its final, magnificent ground on the peaks. Near the Jasper townsite, you’ll find the park emerging from its slumber. Wildlife like elk, bighorn sheep, and even bears are active at lower elevations, grazing on the first green shoots. The local lakes, such as Pyramid and Patricia, are just beginning to shed their winter ice. The true adventure begins on the Icefields Parkway (Highway 93), one of the most stunning drives on Earth. In May, the roadside is alive with meltwater, feeding thundering waterfalls. A stop at Athabasca Falls is essential, as the river roars through its narrow canyon with peak springtime power. Your ultimate destination is the Athabasca Glacier at the Columbia Icefield. May marks the thrilling start of the glacier season. Here, you can board a massive Ice Explorer, a specially designed vehicle that drives directly onto the ancient river of ice. Stepping out, you are standing on ice that is hundreds of feet thick and thousands of years old. The sheer scale is humbling as you stare into deep, blue crevasses and taste pure glacial water.

Athabasca Glacier at the Columbia Icefield

Visiting Jasper in May offers the best of both worlds: the raw power of the spring melt and the icy majesty of winter. Just be sure to pack layers—you’ll experience freezing temperatures on the glacier and mild, spring-like weather in the valley, all in a single afternoon.
